Meal 1: Crispy Tofu With Peanut Sauce

Description:

A velvety blend of peanut butter, ginger, and garlic, thickened with coconut cream, served with crispy tofu cubes. Inspired by satay, this dish is an enormously satisfying weeknight dinner.

What you’ll need:

  • Large Skillet
  • Microplane
  • Wooden Spoon

Ingredients:

  • 4 servings
  • 3 Tbsp. vegetable oil, divided
  • 1 14-oz. package firm tofu, drained, patted dry, cut into ½" cubes
  • 1 large shallot, finely chopped
  • 1 2" piece ginger, peeled, finely grated
  • 5 garlic cloves, finely grated
  • 1 tsp. ground cumin
  • 1 tsp. red chile powder or crushed red pepper flakes
  • ½ tsp. ground turmeric
  • 1 5.4-oz. can unsweetened coconut cream
  • 2 Tbsp. smooth natural peanut butter
  • 1 Tbsp. honey
  • 1 tsp. Diamond Crystal or ½ tsp. Morton kosher salt
  • 1 Tbsp. fresh lime juice
  • Cilantro leaves, thinly sliced jalapeños, steamed white rice, and lime wedges (for serving)

Preparation:

    1. Heat 1 tablespoon of vegetable oil in a large nonstick skillet over medium-high heat. Cook the tofu cubes, turning occasionally, until golden brown and crispy, about 5-7 minutes. Transfer the tofu to a plate.
    2. In the same skillet, heat the remaining 2 tablespoons of vegetable oil over medium heat. Add the shallot, ginger, and garlic. Cook, stirring often, until the shallot is translucent and starting to brown around the edges, about 5-7 minutes.
    3. Add the ground cumin, red chile powder (or crushed red pepper flakes), and ground turmeric to the skillet. Cook, stirring, until the spices are fragrant, about 1 minute.
    4. Add the unsweetened coconut cream, smooth natural peanut butter, and honey to the skillet. Stir to combine. Bring the mixture to a simmer and cook, stirring occasionally, until the sauce is thickened, about 5 minutes.
    5. Whisk in ½ cup of water and the Diamond Crystal or kosher salt. Return the tofu to the skillet and simmer until heated through, about 2 minutes. Thin the sauce with up to ¼ cup of water if needed, stirring to combine.
    6. Remove the skillet from heat and stir in the fresh lime juice.
    7. Scatter cilantro leaves and thinly sliced jalapeños over the saucy tofu. Serve with steamed white rice and lime wedges.
